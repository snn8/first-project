Where Bulgarian history, traditions and nature merge.


By Svetoslava Nacheva

My last trip was to Koprivshtitsa - a small town, famous for its historical importance. It's been years since I last travelled by train in Bulgaria and it is a special kind of experience. The scenery, green forests, high mountain peaks and the wide blue sky - all of that captivates you with its beauty.

My arrival

It was a 2-hour trip by train from Sofia to Koprivshtitsa. The moment I arrived in the town, I was mesmerised by the view - numerous tiny houses surrounded by a magnificent mountain range. I felt insignificant in comparison to nature and its mighty presense. It serves as a reality check.

Day 1

I spent the first day exploring the central town area. I took a long walk by the river which goes through Koprivshtitsa and separates it in two. The sun was shining, the wind was blowing and birds were singing joyfully. Looking at this beautiful scenery, I wanted to capture it with my camera.

Museums

On the second day I visited several museums. As I was crossing the threshold of those old traditional houses, my mind was going back in time and imagining the life people had. I was bombarded with all kinds of emotions. It felt as if the houses were alive and they were sharing their stories with me.

Day 2

The significance of the town comes from its history. Many poets and revolutionaries, who played an important role for Bulgaria's liberation and independence, were born in Koprivshtitsa. Their homes are now turned into museums and attract tourists.

Hill tops

The tiny steep streets were leading me to hidden places. I could easily lose a sense of time and space wandering around and capturing those stunning views with my lenses. Looking at the hill tops nearby, I was propelled to reach them.

My last day

The time passed unnoticed. My last day in Koprivshtitsa was dedicated to the museums that were located on the other side of the river. The last house I visited was up on a hill, revealing a breathtaking view of the whole town. What a better way to end my trip?
